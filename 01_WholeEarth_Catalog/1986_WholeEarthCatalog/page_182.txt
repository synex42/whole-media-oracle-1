Soft Sculpture 
A book to help you turn your wildest fabric fantasies into 
sculpture that won't come apart at the seams. A soft sculp- 
ture can be anything from a silk cactus to a velvet dog to 
a life-size corduroy drum set. Who needs clothes anyway? 
—Jeanne Carstensen [Suggested by Rhoda London]

The Reader’s Digest Complete 
Guide to Needlework 
The title of this book should be changed to The Reader's 
Digest GOOD Guide to Needlework. Though it is not 
complete, the skills are covered with an excellence I’ve 
come to expect from Reader’s Digest how-to books. Tools, 
basic techniques, and instructions are covered thoroughly, 
with sample projects. Recommended jumping-off point for 
a beginning needle worker. 
—Evelyn Eldridge-Diaz 
Fern stitches: Start at upper left and work all rows 
down the canvas. 
If left-harded, turn canvas, start 
in lower right 
corner and work all rows up the canvas 
Rings: These are made 
separately, then sewed 
onto the work wherever 
desired. Wind thread 
around a pencil 15 times, 
then buttonhole over all 
of the strands. (The but- 
tonholing is easier if you 
push the threads up to- 
ward the pencil point.) 
When ring is complete, 
remove from pencil, flat- 
ten, and sew in place.
