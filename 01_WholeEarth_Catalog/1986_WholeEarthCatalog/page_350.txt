Guide to Electronics in The Home 
The more antiquated among us sometimes find it difficult 
to deal with things electronic. Which devices are useful? 
Which of those are best? Consumer Reports (p. 150), at 
its best here, explains it all as it reviews home computers, 
TVs, hifis, radios, tape decks, phones, and alarms. As is 
their custom, the Consumers Union folks don’t comment 
on every model of every brand. They make up for this by 
educating you in the basics so you can, for instance, 
make sense out of specification sheets and salesman 
hype. This is the best general introduction to electronic 
gadgetry this side of the nearest teenage hacker. 
—JB
