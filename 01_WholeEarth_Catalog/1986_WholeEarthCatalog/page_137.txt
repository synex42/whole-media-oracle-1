Creosote is undesirable, not only because it is fuel for 
chimney fires, but for several other reasons. It decreases 
the effective flue diameter of the stack. This reduction is 
most dramatic in smaller stacks. For example, a six-inch 
pipe with a one-half-inch buildup of creosote loses 30 
percent of its area.

Once the burn is completed, dampers in the chimney flue 
are shut and the entire mass radiates heat for the next 
12-24 hours. While the gas flow in the heater moves in 
a downdraft past the heat exchange surface of the heater, 
room air outside the heater moves in an updraft pattern 
along the vertical faces of the heater setting up a cir- 
culating flow of warmed air in the living space. It is from 
the opposing flows of warming heater gases and warmed 
room air that the name contraflow heater is derived.
