EASTERN REDBUD (Cercis canadensis) leaves are de- 
ciduous, broadly ovate to heart-shaped, 3 to 5 inches 
wide, with a pointed tip and smooth margins. Turn yel- 
low in fall. Flowers pinkish to lavender, 0.5 of an inch 
long, in loose clusters of 4 to 8; appear before leaves. 
Pinkish, flattened pods, 2.5 to 3.5 inches long, have 
several seeds about 0.3 of an inch long. Bark reddish 
brown, scaly. Usually small, occasionally to 50 feet, 
with a broad, rounded crown.

Fire in America 
This book concerns fire, ecology, and mankind, and the 
history they have made together in North America.

before, and while this dense volume may easily qualify as 
more than you ever wanted to know about fire regimes, 
fire-fighting techniques, and the history and politics of the 
U.S. Forest Service, it is a fascinating story and well told. 
And if anybody gives out awards for the best dust jacket 
photo, this book gets my vote.
