Keep related words together. The position of the words 
in a sentence is the principal means of showing their 
relationship. Confusion and ambiguity result when words 
are badly placed. The writer must, therefore, bring to- 
gether the words and groups of words that are related in 
thought and keep apart those that are not so related. 
He noticed a large stain in the rug that was right in the 
center. 
He noticed a large stain right in the center of the rug.
