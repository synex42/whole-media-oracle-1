Arnold Newman is famous for portraits that use graphic and symbolic 
elements to suggest what a person does. One of his best-known pho- 
tographs is of the composer Igor Stravinsky, a portrait that Newman 
cropped to its essentials. Newman knew what he wanted to do, but, 
just starting out at the time, he didn’t have the focal length lens he 
needed. He moved back until he had what he wanted in the frame, 
then cropped the photograph later. Newman says that the image 
“echoed my feelings about Stravinsky's music: strong, harsh, but with 
a stark beauty of its own.”
