Ever since Greek architecture was re- 
discovered during the Renaissance, architects 
have tried in vain to find a mathematical curve 
that the Greeks might have used for their Doric 
capitals. It was finally found out that no curve 
of analytic geometry could be used, for the 
good reason that the curve which inspired the 
Greek architects was not a perfect curve, but 
the living forms of the common sea urchins, so 
abundant in the Mediterranean.
