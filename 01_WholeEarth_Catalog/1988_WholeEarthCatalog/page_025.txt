Numerical algorithms are the tools used for “num- 
ber-crunching.” Unfortunately, they’re under-util- 
ized because they have an aura of magic to 
many programmers. 
Numerical Recipes is a cookbook for when you 
want to get a job done, not wade through abstract 
discussions. When you need to find out how to do 
a Fast Fourier Transform for the job due yesterday, 
you'll get a basic grounding in the topic, discussion 
of the alternative techniques, and best of all, tested 
source code you can adapt for your own use. The 
authors’ frequent opinions on when and where to 
use each technique are mostly right on the mark. 
The original Numerical Recipes had examples in 
Fortran and Pascal, and thanks to demand, there’s now 
a C version as well. You can also buy all the example 
code on IBM PC or Macintosh-compatible disks. 
— Bob Murphy
