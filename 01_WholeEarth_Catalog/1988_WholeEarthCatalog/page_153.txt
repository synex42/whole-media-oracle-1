When Gutenberg invented moveable type, he 
found strong resistance to it among the current 
publishing experts— medieval monks transcribing 
the Bible. It seemed that what the monks most 
objected to was that his innovation removed the 
cursive strokes that connected letters within one 
word, breaking up the calligraphy and also sep- 
arating writing from illustration. What Michael 
Green has done in this picture book is to pioneer 
the return to a unified graphic in which the artist 
creates both the typography and the illustration. 
The meditating Macintosh is the pen for this elec- 
tronic manifesto. It promises a change in publica- 
tion design.
