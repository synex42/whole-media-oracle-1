“Gocco” means “child’s play,” accurately descrip- 
tive of the level of expertise required to operate 
this nifty little gadget. And certainly any child in 
your household would be happy to prove it, if only 
the adult inhabitants would quit monopolizing it! 
Essentially the Gocco is a photo screen printer. 
You use flash bulbs to make the screen. The origi- 
nal art needs to contain carbon (most inks and all 
photocopies are carbon-based). There’s a little 
flash bulb housing that sits on top of the press’s 
upper half. When you press it shut, flash bulb light 
passes through a light-transparent film, over the 
artwork, hits the carbon of the artwork, is re-radi- 
ated as infra-red light, which melts the film, thereby 
making the print master. Lift off the flash bulb 
housing, and lift up a clear acetate sheet which sits 
impressive.
