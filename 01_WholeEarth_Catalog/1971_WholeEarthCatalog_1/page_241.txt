Paper 
The other day, while cruising around Bizarre Acres, | came across a 
reciepe (?) for making paper. Now at first, the thought of slogging 
over some soggy pulp to make a few sheets of paper a dull experience 
but just think how nice it would be to paint or write on paper that 
_ 
has been produced and prepared by your own hands. 
: 
EQUIPMENT: A Stanley knife or something equivalent with which 
to chcp up pieces of wood, one large saucepan (a pressure cooker 
would be better but don’t worry if you haven't got one) and a 
rolling pin. 
_ 
INGREDIENTS: 
Quantities of birch, pine or spruce (there’s no 
P| need to hack down a tree because branches and twigs picked up 
from the ground will do just as well)

METHOD: 
Cut the wood into pieces for easy handling, remove 
the bark and chop into small chips. Place these chips into the 
saucepan, add sufficient water and boil until they have been 
_ broken down into fibres of desired length. Be careful 
not to let 
the saucepan run dry. (For the owners of a pressure cooker, cook 
at 15 Ibs pressure). After a few hours you will have a fibrous 
cellulose. At this stage, if you fancy a coloured paper, add a dash 
of a non-toxic dye (a vegetable dye is preferable as its non-chem- 
ical) and stir gently.

and dry over an even heat to remove the excess moisture. When 
you're satisfied that the sheets are dry enough, roll into reels 
and leave to stand. You now have a fine matt paper ideal for all 
your purposes.
