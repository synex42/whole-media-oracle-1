Edgar Cayce appears as a rather improbable and 
reluctant psychic. After 
becoming acquainted with his story it is hard not to wonder at his courage and 
strength, He was an unschooled man—but by no means an unintelligent one. 
A man 
deeply religious in his beliefs, who overcame his own doubts only after an agonizing 
search within himself. 
Perhaps most interesting of all, however, is that once he did resolve his doubts, 
and had committed himself to the unorthodox road of the psychic, he never sought 
the limelight. He preferred instead to let the readings and the results they produced 
become the center of attention.
