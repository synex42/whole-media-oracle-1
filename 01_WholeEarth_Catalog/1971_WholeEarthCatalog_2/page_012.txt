Production of Methane Gas from manure and waste matter. 
By—Harold Bate 
In the whole of nature there is nothing more beautiful than the won- 
derful cycle by which matter is kept in circulation by the compen- 
sating agencies in animal and vegitable life. 
~ When any form of vegitation--including manure--is left exposed to the 
moist air, and in the form of vegitation, has ceased growing, the 
celulose begins to decay-the Oxygen of the air once again unites with 
the Carbon of the woody fibre forming Carbon Dioxide which 
mingles with the atmosphere, whilst the Hydrogen and Oxygen of the 
fibre combine as water and escape unseen into the atm josphere:s 
in the 
form of Aqueous vapour. 
- 
If instead of allowing the maternal to decay away in free contact with 
the atmosphere this action had been checked by keeping the 
material under the surface of water or a sufficient layer of soil to cut 
off the atmospheric Oxygen, a checked process of decay would have 
taken place in which the Oxygen present in the material would 
gradually have combined with Carbon and Hydrogen to form a 
gaseous constituent while some of the Hydrogen and Carbon also 
combing together would have yielded small quatities of such 
gaseous Hydro Carbons as Methane. 
Methane Gas is produced in every manure heap—or compost heap— 
when certain bacteria manifest themselves. 
The composting process is brought to completion by 2 distinct types 
of bacteria. Aerobic (Oxygen bacteria act first taking in ed 2 from 
the air). 
These bacteria produce heat in the manure heap. 
When sufficient Nitrogen has accumulated-the Humus bacteria take 
over. 
The bacteria muliply rapidly between 75.F. and 90.F. and this 
temperature should be maintained. 
The Methane Gas producing process is anaerobic since teh need is 
for Nitrogen and NOT OXYGEN. 
Every manure heap on the farm is producing Methane Gas which is 
being lost in the atmosphere. 
Amonia~Phosphoric Acid-and--Nitrogen is lost in the ground and the 
resulting Humus contains very little of these fertilisers after dilution 
by the air and rain water. 
When the manure and waste matter is enclosed in anair tight 
container-nothing can be lost.

manure for the first few days--then--by building a suitable sontainer
