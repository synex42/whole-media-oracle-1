TRINIDAD'S MODEL CITY 
Drop City and Libre formed a group called Community Matrix in order to contribute pooled design ideas 
to Trinidad, Colorado, the town being on the verge of receiving federal assistance. 
In addition to Baer’s 
low-cost housing projections, Community Matrix conceived this plan: 
Cover 4 city blocks with a climate controlled dome utilizing solar energy. Fuller submitted an estimate 
for a 250 ft. dome of $2 million. In the bitterest of winters it’s tee-shirts and gardens! 
All traffic is underground as well as banks, stores, fire department, parking, police station. 
The city hall and the jail would be inside the dome. As no structural strength is required by walls in this 
dome, a system of moveable partitions would be employed using wood, plastic, glass, mirror and dyed 
cloth both opaque and translucent. 
Trinidad has a defunct brick factory we all encouraged to be reopened as a ceramic factory for the world‘s 
choicest tiles. For demonstration purposes much of the inside of the dome would be tiled: sidewalks, 
benches, fountains, streams. 
The entire ground level inside will be a garden of changes. With climate control, plants of all regions will 
be able to grow simultaneously, contributing oxygen and primal information. 
2 and 3 dimensional visions appear everywhere. Color in its glory will enhance all things. 
Lighting will be computerized to allow a continuous flow of changing light so that no 24 hour period will 
be the same. Color will alter according to the specific relativities exclusive to that day thereby changing 
all the designs in the dome. In addition projections of a great variety would appear in various locations in 
the evening. 
A totally walless and casual restaurant would occupy a choice location in the center of the dome. Tables 
would be constructed of many materials and be totally different formally so that each guest would need 
to find the perfect spot for himself. The menu’s total consideration would be given to health. Also small 
natural juice bars would be scattered in the trees.
