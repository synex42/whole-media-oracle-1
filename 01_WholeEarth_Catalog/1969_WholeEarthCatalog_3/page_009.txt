Tits for Tots 
\’d like to see La Leche League added to the Catalog, right 
in there with Dr. Spock. The League is a non-profit 
Organization of nursing mothers, whose purpose is to 
encourage and counsel other women who want to breast- 
feed their babies. They feel that nursing a baby is an art, 
best learned from someone who has done it. Most women 
these days find their doctors, hospitals, relatives, and 
friends and terrifically bottle-oriented and the League can 
really be a help in standing up against them. 
They publish a “manual”, THE WOMANLY ART OF 
BREASTFEEDING ($3.00 puperback, $4.00 hardcover) 
and a bi-monthly newsletter ($2.00 a year plus 25 cents 
postage) both available from their central office, 
La Leche League !nternational 
9616 Minneapolis Avenue 
Franklin Park, Ill. 60132 
Local League groups in most parts of the USA and in some 
other countries meet to talk over the advantages and the 
problems of breastfeeding and to trade experiences. The 
central office can tell you where the nearest group is or 
can tell you how to start your own; they'll give you 
telephone numbers of League mothers to call for help. 
I'd add from my own experience that nursing a baby is 
a great trip and that the League, though its composition 
varies from place to place, usually attracts interesting 
women whose concern is for PEOPLE more than things 
or images. 
Christine Mulford
