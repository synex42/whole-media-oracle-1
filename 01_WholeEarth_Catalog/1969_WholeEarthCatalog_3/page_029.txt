We’re only in it for the money 
Sometime this Winter the CATALOG should pay 
its debts and climb into the black financially. 
10% of our gross income goes to Portola Instit- 
ute for maintenance of the facility and other 
projects than ours, Most of what’s left goes into 
CATALOG operation. 
That still leaves some. 
We’re non-profit -- in spirit and in tact-so we 
can’t pocket the loot and don’t want to, 
We could lower the price of the CATALOG. We 
could pay reviewers more. We could get more 
staff and better publication tools, We could try 
some stuff. 
We are getting better tools and more staff. For 
now we are not lowering the price of the 
CATALOG further. Nor are we paying review- 
ers more, for reasons that have to do with the 
difference between a college football game and 
a pro football game: this is an amateur publi- 
cation which wishes to remain so. There will 
probably soon be more self-education and 
consumer publications cold-eyed-professional 
in the market and they’re welcome. 
They may 
find it’s a warm-eyed market. 
We are going to try some stuff. 
These will be research and public education projects 
initiated by us to pursue goals and hypotheses generated 
by work on the CATALOG. 
They will be reported in the 
Supplements, and if we’re at all successful they'll be 
reported elsewhere. 
They should help make the CATALOG a 
better publication, less academic, more armpit. 
The projects 
will be as open as we can make them to your participation.

The CATALOG has but 20 months to live 
Plans change, but at this writing our plan is to cease 
publication of the WHOLE EARTH CATALOG in the Spring 
of 1971. At that time we will gather the best of the 
CATALOGs and Supplements and publish a good big 
softcover mass market book, probably distributed by 
some New York house. 
If by that time there aren’t people and ideas around 
doing a better job than us, then we'll have failed: 
we

be obsolescent and in the way, and our departure will be 
occasion for sighs of relief and a party.
