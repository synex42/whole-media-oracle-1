Low-cost paper napkins are about 20 cents 
for a package of eighty. (The only cheaper 
ones are restaurant packages of five hundred.) 
That means the cost per napkin is about 1/4 
cent. If you have four people at table, that 
comes to about 1 cent per meal, 3 cents per 
day, 21 cents per week. 
Now you're in a position to figure a 
comparative cost. If the cloth napkins last 
a year and cost 50 cents each, their average 
cost per week is about 1 cent each, or 4 cents 
per week for your family of four. The washing 
adds another 4 cents per week, so your total 
cloth-napkin costs are 8 cents per week. 
Now you know why grandma, who isn’t so 
dumb and doesn’t have any spare cash to 
speak of, uses cloth napkins.
