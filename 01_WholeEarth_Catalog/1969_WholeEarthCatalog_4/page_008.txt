One night when | had tasted bitterness | went out on to the hill. Dark 
heather checked my feet. Below marched the suburban street lamps. 
Windows, their curtains drawn, were shut eyes, inwardly watching the 
lives of dreams. Beyond the sea’‘s level darkness a lighthouse pulsed. 
Overhead, obscurity. 
From all the coincident and punctual centres of power, light leapt and 
blazed. The cosmos exploded, actualizing its potentiality of space and 
time. The centres of power, like fragments of a bursting bomb, were 
hurled apart. But each one retained in itself, as a memory and a longing, 
the single spirit of the whole; and each mirrored in itself aspects of all 
others throughout all the cosmical space and time.
