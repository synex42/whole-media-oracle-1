Friction is of far greater importance in statics than 
is often realized. Without it no ladder could stand 
against a wall, a two-legged animal could hardly 
remain upright, and a large number of structures 
would become unstable. Most simple one- or two- 
family houses are set loosely on their foundations 
and depend on their weight and friction to stay there. 
Without friction, such houses would be blown away 
by the slightest breeze. Worse than that, without 
friction, all houses, animals, and men who were not 
standing on solid rock, but on sand, clay, or dirt, 
would sink down in it until Archimedes’ law restored 
equilibrium through buoyancy.
