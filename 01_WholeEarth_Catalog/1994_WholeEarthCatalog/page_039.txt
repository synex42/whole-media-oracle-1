In Search of the Miraculous: Fragments of an Unknown Teaching 
No discussion of modem spirituality 
would be complete without a glimpse 
of G.. Gurdjieff, the mysterious Greco- 
Armenian master who informed the 
Western world early in this century 
that the mental state we go around in 
every day is literally a “waking sleep.” 
In Search of the Miraculous, by 
Gurdjieffs student P.D. Ouspensky, 
gives a lucid account of Gurdjieff’s 
unique, provocative style of spiritual 
instruction and his view of “waking up’ 
as well as a grand and unique view of 
the cosmos and humanity's part in it. 
The ideas here range from the inspir- 
ing to the perplexing, but practically 
every page has the power to jolt one 
from the slumber of daily life. 
—Richard Smoley
