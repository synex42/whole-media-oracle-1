Pickleberry Pie 
This marvelous half-hour program is 
heard weekly on about eighty public 
radio stations, and twice daily in some 
two hundred cities (including Mexico 
and Europe) via Digital Cable Radio. 
The show's central conceit is the 
Pickleberries — “tiny, bumpy, green 
creatures” who sound like hamsters on 
helium and who interview kids and 
guest musicians. 
The Pickleberries often embody the 
foibles and conflicts of their young lis- 
teners, making this program a 
uniquely sensitive offering, but the 
program's core is as a showcase for 
the best in children's music. 
The result is thoroughly delightful for 
three- to seven-year-olds; when my
