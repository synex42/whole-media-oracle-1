A lot of rocks look alike. Geologists work by 
terrains. Out of context, a volcanic rock 
may actually appear to be a sedimentary 
rock. The fieldguides with color photos can 
help (the best is Rocks and Minerals), but 
they can often lead to much confusion. It's 
best to know the terrain you're in — use 
the Roadside Geology series, the Geologi- 
cal Highway Maps, or local maps from the 
state or fed geological survey.
