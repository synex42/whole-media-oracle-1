life’s 3.9-billion-year history on the planet Earth; 
the process that creates biodiversity. The twen- 
tieth century added stunning discoveries to 
Darwin’s legacy. 
* The red-in-tooth-and-claw boys confronted 
symbiosis. The evolutionary tree just does not 
always branch; sometimes the branches touch 
and join. Lynn Margulis says it best: Natural 
selection is an editor; symbiosis a major author.

now all Africans by lineage. A few details have 
evolved in new directions but, in evolutionary 
essence, we are all African primates.
