Very moist air is usually of great help to a 
child having a croup attack. Moist air re- 
lieves the swelling in the larynx and opens 
up the airway. The child will begin to 
breathe more easily almost immediately. 
Children often become upset by the fact 
that they cannot stop coughing. Parents 
should encourage the child to be calm (and 
be calm themselves) because this will help 
to break the cycle of coughing. The child 
should be told to breathe slowly and 
evenly. Many children relax when they are 
told that moist air will stop the coughing 
and make the breathing easier. If there is 
any question of difficulty breathing, call the 
doctor. 
Moist air can be generated quickly by run- 
ning a cool mist vaporizor or humidifier in a 
small room, close to the child; by running a 
hot shower; or by running a sinkful of hot 
water and holding the child over it. If hot 
water is run in the sink, a towel or a piece 
of plastic can be used to create a makeshift
