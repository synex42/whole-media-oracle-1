Understanding the Sky 
There is no better way to feel out local 
weather than in a small airplane (or 
reincamated as a red-tailed hawk). 
This is the most lucid book on the 
messages of the clouds, the patterns 
of the local winds, thunderstorms, and 
thermal lore. The life-or-death nature 
of the information has produced good 
prose and great illustrations. Pagen 
also includes the big picture — how 
storm fronts carry your watershed 
news from the arctic or the equator. 
—PW
