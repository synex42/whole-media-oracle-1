Earthships 
aesthetic tends to the unconventional. 
It's fair to call them affordable, but 
they're not for everyone. The hubris 
is wonderful. 
As defined — and built! — here, an 
Earthship is of the earth, heated and 
cooled by natural systems, raising its 
own food, managing its own water 
supply, and not making a mess, while 
providing good shelter for its occu- 
pants, It is made by hand from simple 
materials, including detritus of our in- 
dustrial society — tires and cans, for 
instance. Earthships demonstrate that 
the result need not be funky like six- 
ties hippy hovels, though obviously the 
Volume | lays out the basic vision. Vol- 
ume II adds spiritual and engineering 
details. Volume Ill shows more details, 
what's been leamed as the number of 
Earthships grows, and what the poten- 
tial might be. Earthships have become 
a veritable movement, and an inter- 
esting one at that. To my mind, the 
Earthship spirit is more important 
than the ships themselves. —JB
