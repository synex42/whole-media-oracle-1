Building with Junk 
and Other Good Stuff 
This book may be offensive to banks 
and developers; its anarchic spirit 
encourages individual action and sass. 
The author is a master scrounger 
(recycler in ecospeak) and make-do 
person, yet he is aware of energy 
matters as well as unhealthy mat- 
erials and building practices. His 
writing makes you feel like you're 
hatching a plot with your Cousin Jim. 
Nothing illegal, but he's certainly out 
to circumvent and exploit The System. 
The advice is thorough, true, and full 
of droll wit. Before you give up on that 
unaffordable dream house, better 
give this a read. —B
