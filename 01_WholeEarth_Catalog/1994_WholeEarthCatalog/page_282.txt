Just as a good editor of prose ruthlessly 
prunes out unnecessary words, so a de- 
signer of statistical graphics should prune 
out ink that fails to present fresh data-in- 
formation. Although nothing can replace a 
good graphical idea applied to an interesting 
set of numbers, editing and revision are as 
essential to sound graphical design work as 
they are to writing. T. S. Eliot emphasized 
the “capital importance of criticism in the 
work of creation itself. Probably, indeed, 
the larger part of the labour of an author in 
composing his work is critical labour; the 
labour of sifting, combining, constructing, 
expunging, correcting, testing: this frightful 
toil is as much critical as creative.”
