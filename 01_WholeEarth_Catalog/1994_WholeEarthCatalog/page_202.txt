Delta of Venus 
After fifty years, Delta of Venus is one 
of the hottest books around. 
Particularly enchanting are the diary 
entries at the beginning of the book 
that document the evolution of Nin’s 
pomographic style. “Less poetry,” the 
anonymous patron who subsidizes 
Nin’s forays into smut-mongering 
keeps telling her. At first, Nin is a 
dutiful hack. But ultimately, she rebels, 
“Sex loses all its power and magic 
when it becomes explicit, mechanical, 
overdone, when it becomes a mecha- 
nistic obsession,” she tells him. 
“It becomes a bore.” 
And so pomography acquired a 
female sensibility: herotica was born!
