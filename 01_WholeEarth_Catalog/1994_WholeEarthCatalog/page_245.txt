The paper feed cams, which pick up the 
paper in laser printers and copiers, are 
covered with textured rubber that lifts the 
paper and passes it through the machine. 
With age and use, the rubber on the cams 
may harden and lose its grip. Thus, the 
printer will miss the page pickup, causing 
the top of the print to appear halfway 
down the page. Wiping these rollers with 
acetone may revitalize the rubber and 
revive the grip. It is important not to use 
rubbing alcohol, because it will dry out 
the rubber. —The Underground Guide
