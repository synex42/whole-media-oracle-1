red-bloodedness that seemed inappropriate to those fulfilling the 
nurturing role.”

Some grasslands seem to vacillate between 
being grasslands and being forests, The 
agent that tips the balance in these cases Is 
fire. Most trees are killed or weakened by 
fire, but grasses have adapted to survive it 
tion. Fire removes litter (dead plant mate- 
rial), which would otherwise shade the soil 
and keep it from warming up in the early 
spring. Furthermore it releases the nutri- 
| ents that are locked up in the litter. The 
grasses thus begin to grow earlier in the 
season, and they reach greater heights.
