Beadwall (Patent Applied for) 
There has been a real need fora way to let in sunlight while 
it’s shining, and then insulate the window to hold the heat. 
David Harrison has come up with the Beadwall. 
Tiny 
styrofoam pellets are blown into the space between two 
layers of glazing to insulate it, and sucked out when you want 
to admit solar radiation. He worked with Steve Baer and 
SOLAR HARDWARE 
Zomeworks until the idea was developed enough to be 
practical. 
It works! It’s strikingly beautiful too, as it fills 
and empties. The possibilities opened by the Beadwall are 
interesting, particularly for greenhouses. Plans show 
system basics, recommend components, specify sizes and 
materials and offer considerable detail. A license to make 
one is included.

Steve Baer’s now-famous heat storage wall fashioned from 
55 gallon drums. One end of the drums face south and 
catch solar heat. At night or during no-sun, large doors 
cover and insulate the weather side. The other end of the 
drums radiate stored heat into the room. Simple, cheap, 
and effective. 
Plans show considerable detail, and include 
systems incorporating Beadwall and Skylids instead of the 
doors.
