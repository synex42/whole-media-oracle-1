A slender perennial vine, woody at the base, with 4-inch-long 
heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are white, in large clusters 
1-1/2 inches wide. The fruit is a one-seeded capsule. Native 
to tropical America. 
Cultivation and Propagation: 
Cultivate much like the 
morning glory (which see) except.that it is less hardy and 
should be started indoors in the north. 
It will also take 
more water than the common morning glory and, as it is 
somewhat woody, it can be cut back and brought indoors 
to carry it over the winter in the North. 
Harvesting: The seeds should be gathered as the pods turn 
brown and dry.
