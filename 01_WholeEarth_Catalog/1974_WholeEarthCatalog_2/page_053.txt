The first pickings of any plant are the choicest in flavour and 
are nutritionally superior to subsequent pickings. The 
variations in flavour between the first and last pickings 
of certain fruits, particularly strawberries, confirm this. 
Studies have shown that their vitamin C content drops 
about 50-per cent in the first two weeks of picking. The 
sweetness of sugar in the first strawberries is disguised by 
the tartness of the high ascorbic acid content. As more 
and more berries are produced leaving less and less ascorbic 
acid in the plant the sweetness of the flavour then pre- 
dominate in the later pickings. Those who prefer such 
smaller later pickings of strawberries, because of their 
sweetness, do not realize that they have lost much vitamin 
C. Such marked flavour differences suggest marked 
nutritional differences. Jam makers are cognizant of the 
exceptional jelling quality of the first berries in contrast 
with later pickings. The latter often will not jell without 
the addition of some kind of pectin.-.
