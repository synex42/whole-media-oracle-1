WARNING COLORS 
Many brightly colored insects, particularly larvae, show 
themselves openly and are conspicuous from afar. These 
insects do advertise their presence with warning colors, for 
they enjoy the protection of distasteful, even toxic, body 
fluids. There are many examples. Caterpillars that feed on 
poisonous herbs may carry the toxic effect through + 
their 
development even to the mature form. The blister beetles 
(Meloidae) contain cantharidin, a toxin dangerous to man 
and beast. Many caterpillars are clothed with urticating 
(stinging) hairs or with thorns, some of which are filled 
with acid. Certain larval moths (Dirphia) imitate this kind 
of caterpillar not only in the coloration and specific hair 
pattern of the abdomen, but also in the contortions EDs 
body goes through.
