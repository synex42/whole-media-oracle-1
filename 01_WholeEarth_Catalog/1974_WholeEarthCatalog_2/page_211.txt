Through use of dried foods and those naturally low in water 
content, shucking cardboard packaging, and moderately 
careful menu planning, it is possible to feed the average hiker 
to repletion on 2 pounds of food per day. By more precise 
planning the job can be done with 1-1/2 pounds. And if the 
party members are willing to leave a bit of lard along the way 
and endure pangs while stomachs shrink, with 1 pound. At 
this point, though, about the fifth evening the hiker will look 
into the setting sun and see not the majestic drama of day’s
