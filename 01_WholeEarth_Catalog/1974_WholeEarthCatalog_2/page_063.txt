Because there has not been any coherent theory of the 
nature of design, and because it is evident that what a thing 
does has some bearing on what it looks like, ‘function’ has 
been loosely used to cover any or all the factors which limit 
preference. It has diverted attention from the fact that those 
influences are many, disparate, and of various effect, and 
Particularly from the fact that economy, not physics, is 
always the predominant influence because directly and 
indirectly it sets the most limits. ‘Function’ apparently 
covers economy as well as anything else you please. It is 
a wonderful hindrance to any understanding of design and 
will die hard, for it makes a fairly intricate subject look 
simple.
