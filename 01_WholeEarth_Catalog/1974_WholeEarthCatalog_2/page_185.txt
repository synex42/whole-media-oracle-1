China and the Chinese are a marvel. There is no doubt that 
the next century will be theirs. They started with nothing but 
People, and are still building in a direction which makes 
Sense to them. In the process they have learned much, made 
Many mistakes and moved on. One wonders if Mao’s prophecy

unthinkable apocolypse of the future in our own country.

Recycling in China 
After making a recent trip to China, Pete Seeger 
commented on the Chinese system of waste re- 
cycling. Apparently the Chinese can bring all of 
their used items to a Waste Products Purchasing 
Center. There is a list price for everything. Old 
pairs of sneakers or worn out bicycle inner tubes 
bring a certain amount of money for each pound 
of rubber; old toothbrushes bring approximately 
a penny apiece because new bristles are put in 
them; & empty toothpaste tubes are purchased 
for the lead in them. Almost everything is 
recycled. 
In addition to the waste purchasing 
plants people connected to the plants go 
through the streets of the different neighbor- 
hoods to purchase recyclable products. 
Recyclable products also include paper, metal, 
glass, and plastics.
