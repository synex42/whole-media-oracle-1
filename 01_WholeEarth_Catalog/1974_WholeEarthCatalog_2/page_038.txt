There are four ways of obtaining a community water supply 
in rural areas: 
(1) tapping and treating raw surface water; 
(2) purchase of treated surface or ground water; (3) 
construction of a single high-capacity water well; and (4) 
construction of several water wells of medium to low 
capacity. Each method offers its own economic and 
operational advantages and each must be thoroughly 
examined before a choice is made. A typical procedure 
is to compare all sources of available water, their costs, 
and their inherent limitations. 
A key feature of the first three alternatives is that they all - 
involve costs which are likely to be prohibitive for low- 
income residents. Tapping and treating raw surface water 
is an expensive operation requiring extensive pumping, 
piping, and treatment facilities. The monthly cost per — 
customer may not be too great if a very large population 
_ 
will be served and the water system can take advantage of — 
economies of scale. 
In a rural area with a relatively small 
population, however, the monthly cost will often be more 
than many residents can afford.
