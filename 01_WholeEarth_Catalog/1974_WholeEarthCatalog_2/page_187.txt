file in the Coast and Geodetic Survey Archives, and copies 
of these as well as original maps and aerial photographs 
(from 1927 on) are available to the public.

The surveys represent a unique and comprehensive record 
of the United States coastline and adjacent waters, showing 
conditions existing at any particular date over more than 
a century and providing a quite detailed record of the 
changes that have occurred from both natural and man- 
made causes during this time. 
If you are interested in 
studies of the shoreline and adjacent land areas, topographic 
surveys should be requested; such surveys are identified by a 
number with the prefix T. Indexes of these surveys are 
available and will be furnished free upon request.
