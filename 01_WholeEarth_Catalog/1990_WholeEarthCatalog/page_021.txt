People of the Tropical Rain Forest 
Visualize a tropical rain forest and one of the last 
things you're likely to imagine is people. Yet it is 
people — both in the tropics and in cities far away 
— who are building roads, logging trees and 
making forests disappear. By explaining how 
people live in these forests worldwide, this book 
makes the enormous problems of rain-forest pres- 
ervation comprehensible. It’s one of the great 
environmental challenges of our age, right up 
there with nuclear annihilation and acid rain. 
From the Yanomami in Brazil to the recent trans- 
migrant settlers in Indonesia, this book examines 
forest dwellers old and new. Over twenty scholars 
contribute essays, and the color photographs, 
from a Smithsonian exhibit, are first-rate. 
—Richard Nilsen
