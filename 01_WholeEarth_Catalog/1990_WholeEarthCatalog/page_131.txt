Esther Ryan 
Not only were we to use recycled paper, we were going to add to our credibil- 
ity by minimizing our oh-so-typical California-style commuting. That part 
proved to be easy; my own driving was reduced from the 20,000 miles of 
previous catalog productions to less than 1800 miles in six months! But there 
was a price to be paid. Despite the wonders of electronic information-han- 
dling, doing a production away from the main office interrupts subtle commu- 
nications that are much faster, simpler and more accurate face-to-face. We 
also found that working at home is fraught with distractions. I’m a land 
steward where | live, and there were emergencies. Add an unusually pestifer- 
ous swarm of small, time-sucking problems that don’t often arrive simultane- 
ously, but did this production, and we had trouble meeting our deadline — 
something that doesn’t happen very often around here. The rest of the Whole 
Earth crew — bless them — stopped production on the next issue of the 
magazine, and came to our rescue at the last minute. Thanks. —JB 
We traditionally do a tree budget — how many trees were killed to produce 
an edition of our book. With recycled paper this is hard to calculate, so play it 
safe: plant a tree for each book you buy, including this one (see p. 20).
