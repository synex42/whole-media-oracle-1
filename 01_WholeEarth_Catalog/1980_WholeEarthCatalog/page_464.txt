that a book like this one is thoroughly a shock. Every 
page yields fresh information (did you know that the 
comic strip was singlehandedly invented by a Swiss gent 
named Tépfler in the 1820's?) and worthwhile hypoth- 
eses about how art and artists gradually teach themselves 
energies of effect. Furthermore the book is a bargain — 
it has 319 fine illustrations, 18 in color.
