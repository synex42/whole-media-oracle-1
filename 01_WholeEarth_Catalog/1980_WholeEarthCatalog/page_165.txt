If an item does not warrant special tooling, there may be 
an economic advantage in using the master part system. 
The system, as the name implies, uses a simulated or 
actual part fabricated to slightly closer tolerances than 
the intended product and used as a template or jig. 
When a master part is used as a jig for locating holes, the 
master and the part to be worked are clamped together 
as shown, and the hole locations are transferred from the 
master to the part with a duplicating punch. 
If possible, 
hardened bushings are inserted into the master part, 
making it possible to transfer holes directly by drilling. 
The addition of drill bushings improves accuracy and 
increases tool life.
