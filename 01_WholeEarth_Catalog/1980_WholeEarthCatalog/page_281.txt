What are the qualities of silk that make it so esteemed? 
Paramount is its beauty: the natural beauty of the fibre, 
and the extraordinary colours it takes. Not only is it 
beautiful, it’s also useful: silk makes the most comfort- 
able clothes, and silk, while seemingly delicate, is actually 
one of the strongest, toughest fibres. And, too, silk is not 
the most abundant of stuffs. 
It takes proverbial effort 
and care to produce, process and weave it. 
Silk is the most lustrous of the natural fibres. This lustre 
is probably its most well known attribute. The lustre 
comes from the way silk is formed. 
It is spun by the silk 
caterpillar as a semi-liquid, continuous filament that 
hardens on contact with air. The smooth surface of 
the silk fibre reflects light, and the reflected light is 
seen as lustre.
