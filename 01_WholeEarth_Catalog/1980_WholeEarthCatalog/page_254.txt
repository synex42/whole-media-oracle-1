Ona new marking gauge the steel pin that scribes the line 
into the wood is rounded down to a point. When scrib- 
ing across the grain, the rounded point scratches the 
fibers and makes a fuzzy line. Therefore the pin of every 
new marking gauge must be refiled. 
If you are right-handed, the edge that will face you when 
you pull the tool should be filed as sharp as a knife. 
If 
you are left-handed, you can reverse the filing to make 
the gauge easier for you to use. 
The difference that refiling makes is easy to see. The 
round point makes a fuzzy line (top), while the refiled 
point makes a crisp, clear line (bottom) with no tendency 
to wander with the grain.
