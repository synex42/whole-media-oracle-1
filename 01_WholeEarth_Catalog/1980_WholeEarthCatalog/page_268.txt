Keeping the knife edge sharp is vital to its effective perfor- 
mance and long life. Only the blade adjacent to the lock- 
nut is ground with a knife edge (learn to recognize it and 
respect it). This blade can be resharpened in seconds by 
honing firmly with a fine-grit whetstone or sharpening 
stone in the manner shown. 
CAUTION: 
All honing must be done on the outside bevel 
surface of the knife edge (the inside surface must not be 
disturbed). Grasp shears firmly as illustrated, avoiding 
finger contact with the knife edge. 
Hold sharpening stone 
against the outside bevel surface of the knife edge at the 
steepest angle which will permit contact with the extreme 
edge. With firm upward strokes hone the edge, working 
from the tip of the blade to the shank with overlapping 
strokes. 
Proper honing will create a continuous burr 
along the inside cutting edge which can be removed 
simply by closing the sharpened shears. 
It is important 
that both cutting edges then be wiped clean before 
further use.
